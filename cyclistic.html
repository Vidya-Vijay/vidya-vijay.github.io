Pedaling Toward Insights: Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis

Google Analytics Capstone Project: Turning ride data into actionable marketing and operational strategies

SQL Power BI DAX Data Analysis

Project Summary

Analyzed Q1 2020 Cyclistic bike-share data to uncover differences between casual riders and members, identify usage patterns, and inform targeted marketing and operational improvements.

  • Focus: Converting casual riders into loyal members
  • Tools: SQL Server for data cleaning and analysis, Power BI for interactive dashboards
  • Data SOurce: Q1 2020 trip data from Cyclistic

Business Context

Cyclistic needed data-driven insights to increase member subscriptions and optimize bike distribution. Understanding ride behavior patterns was essential for improving both operational efficiency and marketing ROI.

Problem Statement

Identify trends in trip volume, ride duration, time-of-day usage, and station activity to develop strategies for:

  • Converting casual riders into members
  • Optimizing bike availability
  • Supporting data-driven marketing campaigns

Tools & Methods

  • Data Cleaning & Analysis: SQL Server Management Studio (SSMS)
  • Visualization:Power BI (DAX, custom visuals)
  • Skills Applied:Data wrangling, aggregation, time-series analysis, data storytelling

Approach

1. Imported and cleaned raw CSV datasets in SQL Server.

2. Wrote SQL queries to compute summary statistics, trip durations, and station-level trends.

3. Built an interactive Power BI dashboard with DAX measures and custom visuals.

4. Generated actionable insights and recommendations to drive business decisions.

Jan–Mar Trends

Weekday vs Weekend

Members peak midweek, casual riders peak weekends

  • Members account for 88.64% of rides (378K trips), and casual riders only 11.36% (48K trips).
  • Member usage dominates each month, with January leading at 136K rides.
  • Casual ridership is relatively low but shows growth from January (8K) to March (28K), hinting at seasonal patterns or warming weather.
  • Casual riders take longer trips (20.3 min average) vs. members (10.5 min), suggesting leisure usage.
  • Members peak midweek (commuting patterns), casual riders peak on weekends (recreational usage).
  • Start time analysis reveals two strong peaks for members (8 AM & 5 PM) and flat usage for casuals, with slight afternoon activity.

Business Recommendations

1. Target casual riders with weekend leisure promotions and convert them into members.

2. Increase bike availability in key stations during weekday peaks and weekend leisure times.

3. Focus marketing efforts on stations with high casual riders activity.
